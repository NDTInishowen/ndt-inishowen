INPP Neuro Developmental Therapy

Neuro-developmental therapists specialize in identifying and treating physical factors that contribute to specific learning difficulties, underachievement and behavioral problems.

INPP (The Institute for Neuro-Physiological Psychology) has been a pioneer in researching the effects of neuro-developmental immaturity on learning and behavior since its establishment in 1975.

The organization continues to study the impacts of retained primitive reflexes and has successfully assisted numerous children worldwide.

Every child is born with a set of primitive reflexes that should be inhibited or controlled by a higher part of the brain within the first year of life.

Failure to inhibit these reflexes can hinder subsequent motor development, visual functioning necessary for tasks like reading and writing and eye-hand coordination. Additionally, it can lead to behavioral challenges, frustration, fear, anxiety, poor concentration and impulse control issues.

As an INPP Neuro-Developmental Therapist, I am equipped to identify underlying causes and provide a drug-free, non-invasive movement program to help your child overcome these challenges.

This program aims to support your child in reaching their full potential by addressing these issues.

The Institute for Neuro-Physiological Psychology

Specialists in identifying and treating physical factors underlying specific learning difficulties, under-achievement and behavioural problems, INPP has pioneered research into the effects of neuro-motor immaturity on learning and behaviour.

Every child is born with a set of primitive reflexes, (sometimes known as "survival reflexes"), which should be inhibited or controlled by a higher part of the brain during the first year of life.

If these reflexes persist beyond the first year, they can interfere with subsequent motor development, visual functioning needed for reading, eye-hand co-ordination needed for writing, and perceptual skills. They provide evidence of immaturity in the functioning of the central nervous system which can act as a barrier to learning. They can also lead to behavioural problems such as concentration difficulties, frustration, poor impulse control and under-achievement.

The INPP Method has been developed to identify underlying causes and to offer a completely drug-free and non-invasive treatment, which can help children overcome these problems and give them the tools with which to succeed.

INPP was established in 1975 and since that time has helped many thousands of children in many countries worldwide.

Details of published research based on The INPP Method may be found at www.inpp.org.uk/publications
